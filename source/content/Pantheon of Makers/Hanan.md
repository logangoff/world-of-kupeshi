Hanan (pronounced Hah-non), the god of creation, destruction, tyrants, and order, is the creator of [[Kupeshi]], a member of the [[Pantheon of Makers]] and the only [[True God]] commonly known. Not much is known of Hanan due to his lack of active influence on the world after [[The Origin Myth of Kupeshi|shunning Kupeshi during the early events of the world]]. His worship is very rare and, if openly done, is typically seen as a strange if not dangerous worship.

## History
Hanan predates all known history and cosmology. Traces of his work exist not only in Kupeshi but in adjacent planes, though those too bear signs of his abandonment as if his rejection of Kupeshi extended outward like a wave, or as if he turned away from an entire web of connected worlds rather than just one. Some scholars argue this means other worlds exist beyond Kupeshi's planar web, worlds where Hanan may still be watching.

No public temples to Hanan have survived the Age of Firsts intact. What exists are fragments of foundation stones, empty canvases found in strange places, and -- most significantly -- the Unfinished Codex, a long lost text of disputed authenticity that some claim to record Hanan's only spoken words to The Twins, [[Uji]] and [[Witta]]. Some believe this codex may contain a method to regain the gaze of Hanan, or his fury.

## Appearance
Hanan has no canonical appearance and no iconography that depicts a face. Worshippers and cultists argue vigorously about this. The most widespread belief among his rare faithful is that he simply cannot be depictedl that any representation of him is, by definition, inadequate, as he is the one who made the capacity for sight and cannot himself be contained by it. Ancient carvings in deep places show only an empty circle.

## Worship
Hanan's worship is extremely rare and regarded with suspicion or hostility by most people. Those who do revere him typically do so quietly and privately, though some minor groups have formed:

1. **The Archivists** are the closest thing to orthodox worship of The Painter. Low in number, pacifist, quiet, scholarly, and often solitary, this group of worshipers is typically separated by great distances and rely on magical communication and messagers to keep in contact with one another. They typically believe Hanan created a perfect world and that we should be penitant for the sins of our ancestors - [[The 4 Sinners]] and The Twins, and not expect the gaze to return. Their practice centers on craft as prayer, making beautiful things and offering them to the empty sky.

2. **The Gaze-Seekers** (sometimes called the Web Cult or the Pathfinders) are the most unusual and largest sect. They believe that Kupeshi and its planes are sealed within a world web, a cluster of creation that Hanan sealed off when he turned away. They believe if they can find or make a passage out of the web, then they will find new worlds, other creations, and eventually the gaze of Hanan where they can then treat for the return of Hanan's favor on the world. They are not violent by doctrine, rather depending on economic and political pressure or blackmail, but relying on violence when it is the shortest path. Their expeditions into deep or forbidden planes make them unpredictable and poorly understood. They collect maps obsessively.

3. **The Flamebearers** are the cult that most fear, and what most believe Hanan worshipers all are. Their theology holds that the only way to regain Hanan's gaze is to punish him for turning his back to the world, and the only way to punish the god is to destroy his creations. They want to end the world to force Hanan's gaze back. Some factions within them have recently been caught delving into old religious vaults, searching for some forgotten location regarding the early world.
